# Entity Separation Strategy — Debt vs. Equity

## Overview

Capital Edge Management, Inc. and NexusBridge Lending LLC operate in separate regulatory lanes. This document defines which products, licenses, and content belong to each entity and governs all website, marketing, and compliance decisions.

---

## The Core Rule

| Entity | Lane | License |
|---|---|---|
| Capital Edge Management, Inc. | **Equity** | Real Estate License |
| NexusBridge Lending LLC | **Debt** | Lending License |

These lanes must never cross. No equity investment products on the NexusBridge site. No lending or debt products on the CEM site.

---

## Corporate Structure

```
Capital Edge Management, Inc. (CEM)
    └── Obsidian & Co. Holdings, LLC
            ├── NexusBridge Capital LP   ← private credit fund (Reg D / 506(c))
            └── NexusBridge Lending LLC  ← lending platform
```

CEM manages both entities but each operates under its own license and regulatory framework.

## CEM Credit Fund — Migration Rule

The Credit Fund currently displayed on capitaledgeinvest.com (Asset-Backed Lending, GAP Funding, Micro-Lending) consists of **lending strategies** — not equity investments. These must be:

1. **Removed** from capitaledgeinvest.com
2. **Maintained** exclusively on nexusbridgelending.com under NexusBridge Capital LP
3. **Referenced** on the CEM site with a cross-link to NexusBridge for lending products

## Why This Matters

Mixing equity and debt products under the same brand or license creates regulatory exposure:

- **Reg CF** (crowdfunding) and **Reg D** (private placement) have different investor eligibility rules, solicitation restrictions, and filing requirements
- A **real estate license** authorizes different activities than a **lending license** — operating outside your licensed scope is a compliance violation
- Commingling fund solicitations across entities can trigger securities law issues

Keeping the two brands clean protects both licenses and ensures each entity's compliance obligations remain clearly scoped.
