## SECTION 2 — RISK FACTORS

**AN INVESTMENT IN THE FUND INVOLVES SIGNIFICANT RISKS. PROSPECTIVE INVESTORS SHOULD CAREFULLY CONSIDER THE FOLLOWING RISK FACTORS, TOGETHER WITH ALL OTHER INFORMATION CONTAINED IN THIS MEMORANDUM, BEFORE MAKING AN INVESTMENT DECISION. THE FOLLOWING LIST OF RISK FACTORS IS NOT EXHAUSTIVE AND DOES NOT PURPORT TO BE A COMPLETE EXPLANATION OF ALL RISKS INVOLVED.**

### 2.1 Illiquidity Risk

The Interests are illiquid securities. There is no public market for the Interests and none is expected to develop. Transfers of Interests are subject to significant restrictions, and investors may be unable to sell or otherwise dispose of their Interests. Investors should be prepared to hold their investment for the full term of the Fund and possibly longer. Redemptions are subject to the General Partner's discretion and available liquidity, and the General Partner may suspend or limit redemptions under certain circumstances.

### 2.2 Borrower Default Risk

The Fund's returns depend on the timely payment of interest and principal by borrowers. Borrowers may default on their loan obligations due to financial difficulty, adverse market conditions, insufficient cash flow, inability to refinance, failure to sell the property, or other factors. Default by one or more borrowers could materially and adversely affect the Fund's returns and ability to make distributions.

### 2.3 Collateral Value and Real Estate Risk

The Fund's loans are secured by real property. Real estate values can decline due to economic downturns, changes in local market conditions, oversupply, changes in interest rates, changes in zoning or regulatory requirements, environmental contamination, natural disasters, and other factors. If the value of collateral securing a loan declines below the outstanding loan balance, the Fund may not recover its full investment upon foreclosure. Foreclosure may be costly and time-consuming, and the Fund may incur losses even with collateral protection.

### 2.4 Interest Rate Risk

Changes in prevailing interest rates may affect the Fund's ability to originate loans at attractive rates, the competitiveness of the Fund's loan products, the market value of the Fund's loan portfolio, and borrowers' ability to refinance or repay their loans at maturity. Rising interest rates may reduce property values and increase borrower default risk.

### 2.5 Regulatory and Legal Risk

The Fund and its lending operations are subject to extensive federal, state, and local laws and regulations, including securities laws, lending regulations, usury laws, consumer protection laws, foreclosure laws, and fair lending requirements. Changes in laws or regulations, or the interpretation thereof, could adversely affect the Fund's business, operations, and profitability. The Fund may be subject to regulatory proceedings, investigations, fines, or enforcement actions. Compliance costs may be significant.

### 2.6 Concentration Risk

The Fund's investments will be concentrated in short-duration, asset-backed loans secured by real property. The Fund will not invest in other asset classes. This concentration subjects investors to the risks specific to real estate lending, without the diversification benefits of a multi-asset class portfolio. Additionally, the Fund's geographic focus on the southern United States subjects the portfolio to regional economic, political, and natural disaster risks.

### 2.7 Geographic Concentration Risk

The Fund's loans will be secured by properties located primarily in the southern belt states (Texas, Tennessee, North Carolina, Georgia, Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Arkansas, South Carolina, and Louisiana). The economies, real estate markets, and regulatory environments of these states are subject to localized risks, including hurricanes, flooding, and other natural disasters; regional economic downturns; changes in state usury laws (notably Arkansas's constitutional rate cap); and differences in foreclosure timelines and procedures. Adverse developments in these states could disproportionately affect the Fund.

### 2.8 Key Person Risk

The success of the Fund depends in significant part on the skills, experience, and continued involvement of the principals of the General Partner, including [KEY PERSON NAME(S)]. The loss, incapacity, or departure of one or more key persons could materially and adversely affect the Fund's performance. The Fund does not maintain key person insurance.

### 2.9 Early Stage / No Operating History Risk

The Fund is newly formed and has no operating history. There can be no assurance that the Fund will achieve its investment objectives or generate positive returns. Prospective investors have limited information on which to base their investment decision. The General Partner's prior experience, while relevant, does not guarantee future results.

### 2.10 No Guarantee of Return

There is no guarantee that the Fund will achieve its target returns, make any distributions to investors, or return any invested capital. Investors may lose their entire investment. Past performance of the General Partner, its principals, or similar investment vehicles is not indicative of future results.

### 2.11 Leverage Risk

The Fund may, in the future, use leverage (borrowed funds) to enhance returns or fund operations. The use of leverage amplifies both potential gains and potential losses. In a declining market, leveraged positions may result in losses that exceed the Fund's invested capital. Even if the Fund does not initially employ leverage at the fund level, the Fund's borrowers will be leveraged through their loan obligations.

### 2.12 Construction and Renovation Risk

A portion of the Fund's portfolio will consist of renovation/rehabilitation loans. Renovation projects are subject to construction delays, cost overruns, contractor disputes, permitting issues, supply chain disruptions, and quality deficiencies. Any of these factors could delay the completion and sale or refinancing of the property, which could impair the borrower's ability to repay the loan and result in losses to the Fund.

### 2.13 Competition Risk

The private lending market is competitive. The Fund competes with banks, credit unions, other private lenders, hard money lenders, and institutional investors for loan origination opportunities. Increased competition may reduce the volume, quality, or pricing of available loan opportunities, which could adversely affect the Fund's returns.

### 2.14 Foreclosure Risk

In the event of a borrower default, the Fund may need to foreclose on the underlying property. Foreclosure timelines and costs vary significantly by state. In judicial foreclosure states (such as Florida and South Carolina), foreclosure may take 12–24 months or longer, during which time the Fund may not receive any income from the loan and may incur significant legal and property maintenance costs. The Fund may not recover the full amount of its investment through foreclosure.

### 2.15 Environmental Risk

Properties securing the Fund's loans may be contaminated by hazardous materials or subject to environmental liabilities. The Fund, as a secured lender and potential foreclosure purchaser, could be exposed to environmental liability, cleanup costs, and litigation expenses under federal and state environmental laws.

### 2.16 Tax Risk

The Fund's investments have complex tax implications. Changes in tax laws, regulations, or their interpretation could adversely affect the Fund and its investors. The Fund will be treated as a partnership for U.S. federal income tax purposes, and investors will be taxed on their allocable share of the Fund's income, whether or not cash is distributed. Investors should consult their own tax advisors. Tax-exempt investors should consider the potential for Unrelated Business Taxable Income (UBTI).

### 2.17 Conflicts of Interest Risk

The General Partner and its affiliates may face conflicts of interest in managing the Fund. These conflicts are described in Section 12 below. While the General Partner will endeavor to resolve all conflicts in a fair and equitable manner, there can be no assurance that conflicts will always be resolved in favor of the Fund or its investors.

The Fund targets the private credit market segment that is underserved by traditional banks — borrowers who need fast, reliable capital for time-sensitive real estate transactions and cannot access conventional financing due to the speed, credit profile, or nature of the transaction.

### 4.3 Underwriting Criteria

All loans are underwritten using an institutional-quality process that evaluates:

**(a) Collateral Analysis:**
- Independent third-party appraisal (BPO may be accepted for micro loans under $50,000)
- LTV calculation based on as-is value or ARV (renovation loans)
- Property inspection and condition assessment
- Title search and title insurance
- Environmental review (Phase I, if applicable)
- Insurance verification

**(b) Borrower Analysis:**
- Credit history and score (minimum threshold: [CREDIT SCORE THRESHOLD])
- Real estate investment experience
- Liquidity and reserves
- Track record of prior projects (for renovation loans)
- Background and criminal check
- OFAC / SDN screening

**(c) Transaction Analysis:**
- Loan purpose and exit strategy (sale, refinance, or other)
- Feasibility of exit within the loan term
- Market analysis for the property location
- Comparable sales and rental analysis
- Construction budget review (renovation loans)

**(d) Risk Flags:**
The underwriting process identifies and evaluates risk flags including but not limited to: declining market areas, environmental concerns, title defects, borrower litigation history, excessive leverage, and unrealistic exit strategies.

## SECTION 6 — FEES AND EXPENSES

### 6.1 Management Fee

The General Partner shall receive an annual management fee equal to **two percent (2.00%)** of committed capital (the "Management Fee"). The Management Fee shall be calculated and payable quarterly in advance (i.e., 0.50% per quarter), beginning on the initial Closing Date. The Management Fee compensates the General Partner for fund management, administration, investor relations, and oversight of the lending operations.

### 6.2 Performance Allocation (Carried Interest)

The General Partner shall receive a performance allocation (the "Carried Interest") equal to **twenty percent (20.00%)** of net profits allocated to each limited partner, calculated after the limited partner has received distributions equal to: (a) a return of contributed capital; and (b) a preferred return of **eight percent (8.00%) per annum** (non-compounding) on unreturned contributed capital (the "Preferred Return").

The Carried Interest is subject to a "catch-up" provision, whereby after limited partners have received the Preferred Return, 100% of additional profits are allocated to the General Partner until the General Partner has received 20% of total profits, after which remaining profits are split 80% to limited partners and 20% to the General Partner.

**Waterfall Summary:**

| Priority | Allocation |
|---|---|
| 1. Return of Capital | 100% to Limited Partners |
| 2. Preferred Return (8% per annum) | 100% to Limited Partners |
| 3. GP Catch-Up | 100% to General Partner (until GP has received 20% of total profits) |
| 4. Residual Profits | 80% to Limited Partners / 20% to General Partner |

### 6.3 Origination Fees

Origination fees charged to borrowers in connection with loans originated by the Fund shall be retained by the Fund as income. The General Partner may, in its discretion, allocate a portion of origination fee income to offset fund operating expenses. Origination fees are not additional charges to investors.

### 6.4 Extension and Late Fees

Extension fees, late fees, default interest, and other borrower-paid fees and charges shall be retained by the Fund as income and included in the calculation of net profits for distribution and performance allocation purposes.

### 6.5 Fund Expenses

## SECTION 7 — DISTRIBUTION POLICY

### 7.1 Distribution Frequency

The Fund intends to make distributions to limited partners on a **quarterly** basis, subject to available cash flow and the General Partner's determination that such distributions are prudent and appropriate. The General Partner may, in its discretion, increase or decrease the frequency of distributions, defer distributions, or suspend distributions entirely, based on the Fund's financial condition, liquidity needs, and loan performance.

### 7.2 Sources of Distributions

Distributions may be sourced from: (a) interest income received from borrowers; (b) origination fees, extension fees, late fees, and other borrower-paid charges; (c) principal repayments upon loan maturity or prepayment; and (d) proceeds from foreclosure sales or other liquidation of collateral.

### 7.3 Distribution Waterfall

Distributions shall be made in the following order of priority:

**First:** To limited partners, pro rata in proportion to their capital account balances, until each limited partner has received an amount equal to the return of such limited partner's contributed capital;

**Second:** To limited partners, pro rata, until each limited partner has received the Preferred Return (8% per annum, non-compounding) on unreturned contributed capital;

**Third:** To the General Partner, 100% of available cash flow, until the General Partner has received an amount equal to 20% of the aggregate Preferred Return and catch-up distributions;

**Fourth:** 80% to limited partners (pro rata) and 20% to the General Partner.

### 7.4 Reinvestment

During the Fund's investment period, principal repayments received upon loan maturity or prepayment may be reinvested in new loans rather than distributed to investors, at the General Partner's discretion. This capital recycling is intended to maximize the Fund's deployment and returns.

### 7.5 Tax Distributions

The General Partner may, in its discretion, make special tax distributions to limited partners in amounts sufficient to cover the estimated federal and state income tax liability attributable to each limited partner's share of the Fund's taxable income.

### 7.6 No Guarantee

Distributions are not guaranteed. The Fund's ability to make distributions depends on loan performance, cash flow, and market conditions. Investors should not rely on projected or target returns.

## SECTION 12 — CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

### 12.1 Multiple Activities of the General Partner

The General Partner and its affiliates (including Capital Edge Management, Inc. and its officers, directors, and employees) are not required to devote their full time and attention to the affairs of the Fund. The General Partner and its affiliates may manage other investment vehicles, conduct other business activities, and provide services to other clients, some of which may compete with the Fund for investment opportunities. The General Partner will endeavor to allocate investment opportunities fairly, but there can be no assurance that the Fund will have access to all opportunities that may be suitable for it.

### 12.2 Affiliated Loan Origination

Loans originated by NexusBridge Lending LLC, an affiliate of the General Partner, will be offered to the Fund. The General Partner has a financial interest in originating loans (through origination fees and management fees) that may create an incentive to originate loans of lower quality or on terms that are more favorable to the General Partner than to the Fund. The General Partner will mitigate this conflict by applying consistent, institutional-quality underwriting standards to all loans and by maintaining the Investment Committee approval process.

### 12.3 Related Party Transactions

The General Partner, its affiliates, and their respective officers, directors, members, and employees may engage in transactions with the Fund, including: (a) earning origination fees on loans originated for the Fund; (b) earning management fees for managing the Fund; (c) earning performance allocations on Fund profits; (d) providing services to the Fund for which they are compensated; and (e) co-investing in loans alongside the Fund. All material related party transactions will be disclosed to investors. The General Partner will ensure that the terms of any related party transaction are no less favorable to the Fund than would be available from an unaffiliated third party in an arm's-length transaction.

### 12.4 Other Funds and Vehicles

The General Partner may, in the future, manage additional investment funds or vehicles with investment strategies that overlap with the Fund's strategy. In such cases, the General Partner will adopt an allocation policy to ensure that investment opportunities are allocated fairly among the Fund and other vehicles.

### 12.5 Principal Investments

The General Partner and its principals may invest in the Fund alongside limited partners. While this aligns the interests of the General Partner with those of investors, it also means that the General Partner may have a personal financial interest in certain investment decisions.

### 12.6 Fee Arrangements

The fee structure described in Section 6 creates potential conflicts. The Management Fee (based on committed capital) may incentivize the General Partner to raise additional capital even if attractive investment opportunities are limited. The Performance Allocation may incentivize the General Partner to take greater risks to generate higher returns. These conflicts are inherent in the fund management structure and are mitigated by the General Partner's fiduciary duties, the preferred return hurdle, and the General Partner's reputation and long-term business interests.

### 12.7 Resolution of Conflicts

The General Partner acknowledges its fiduciary duties to the Fund and its limited partners and will endeavor to resolve all conflicts of interest in a fair and equitable manner, consistent with its obligations under the Limited Partnership Agreement and applicable law. When conflicts cannot be avoided, the General Partner will disclose the conflict to affected investors to the extent required by law and the Limited Partnership Agreement.
