## 1.1 Overview of the Fund

NexusBridge Capital LP (the "Fund") is a Delaware limited partnership organized to originate, fund, and manage a diversified portfolio of short-duration, asset-backed loans. The Fund's investment objective is to generate attractive risk-adjusted returns for its limited partners ("Limited Partners" or "LPs") through the origination of bridge loans, renovation/rehabilitation loans, and micro loans secured by real property and other qualifying assets, primarily in the southern belt states of the United States.

Capital Edge Management, Inc. ("CEM" or the "General Partner"), a [STATE] corporation, serves as the sole general partner of the Fund and is responsible for all investment decisions, loan origination, underwriting, servicing, and day-to-day management of the Fund's affairs.

The Fund is a private credit fund. It does not acquire, own, or operate real estate or any other tangible assets for its own account. Rather, the Fund deploys capital as secured loans to borrowers who utilize the loan proceeds for their own purposes, including but not limited to real estate acquisition, construction, renovation, or working capital needs. The Fund's returns are generated from interest income and origination fees on the loans it makes, not from appreciation in property values or rental income.

## 1.2 Investment Strategy Summary

The Fund will originate and manage three primary categories of secured loans:

**Bridge Loans.** Short-term loans with terms of six (6) to twelve (12) months, bearing interest at annual rates of ten percent (10%) to fourteen percent (14%), with origination fees of two (2) to three (3) points. Bridge loans will generally be secured by first-lien positions on real property with loan-to-value ("LTV") ratios not exceeding sixty-five percent (65%) to seventy-five percent (75%) of the appraised value of the collateral.

**Renovation/Rehabilitation Loans.** Short-term loans with base terms of three (3) months (subject to extension), bearing interest at annual rates of twelve percent (12%) to fifteen percent (15%), with origination fees of two (2) to four (4) points. Renovation loans will generally be secured by real property with LTV ratios not exceeding seventy percent (70%) of the after-repair value ("ARV") of the collateral. Loan proceeds may be disbursed in draws based on completion milestones.

**Micro Loans.** Smaller loans ranging from ten thousand dollars ($10,000) to seventy-five thousand dollars ($75,000) with base terms of three (3) months, bearing interest at annual rates of fourteen percent (14%) to eighteen percent (18%), with origination fees of three (3) to five (5) points. Micro loans may be secured by real property or other qualifying assets as determined by the General Partner.

The Fund will focus its lending activities in the southern belt states, including Texas, Tennessee, North Carolina, Georgia, Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Arkansas, South Carolina, and Louisiana. The General Partner believes that these markets present compelling opportunities for private credit deployment due to population growth, economic expansion, strong borrower demand, and pricing dynamics that support attractive risk-adjusted returns.

## 1.5 Private Credit Market Opportunity

The private credit market in the United States has experienced significant growth over the past decade, driven by a widening gap between the financing needs of small and mid-size borrowers and the tightening of traditional bank lending standards. Following the implementation of Dodd-Frank and Basel III capital requirements, commercial banks have significantly reduced their exposure to short-term real estate-secured lending, particularly for non-owner-occupied properties, fix-and-flip projects, and small-balance commercial transactions.

This contraction in bank lending has created a substantial opportunity for non-bank private credit providers to fill the void. Borrowers who require speed, flexibility, and certainty of execution — attributes that traditional bank lending often cannot provide — are willing to pay premium interest rates and origination fees in exchange for reliable, timely capital.

The southern belt states targeted by the Fund are experiencing above-average population growth, job creation, and housing demand, which the General Partner believes will sustain strong borrower demand for private credit throughout the Fund's investment period. The combination of attractive yields, collateral-secured lending, short duration, and favorable demographic trends makes private credit in these markets an attractive opportunity for investors seeking yield-generating exposure to a non-correlated asset class.

### 3.2 Illiquidity of Investment

The Interests have not been registered under the Securities Act or any state securities laws and are subject to significant restrictions on transfer. ### 3.3 Borrower Default Risk

The primary risk of the Fund's lending strategy is the risk that borrowers will fail to repay their loans in accordance with the terms thereof. Borrower defaults may result from a variety of factors, including deterioration in the borrower's financial condition, decline in the value of the collateral securing the loan, general economic conditions, project delays or cost overruns, inability to refinance or sell the underlying property, and other factors beyond the control of the Fund or the General Partner. In the event of a borrower default, the Fund may incur significant losses, including the cost of foreclosure and asset disposition, legal fees, lost interest income, and potential shortfalls upon the sale of the collateral.

### 3.4 Collateral Value Risk

The Fund's loans will be secured by real property and, in some cases, other assets. The value of collateral securing the Fund's loans may decline due to various factors, including changes in local real estate market conditions, physical deterioration of the property, environmental contamination, changes in zoning or land use regulations, natural disasters, general economic downturns, and other factors. # SECTION IV — INVESTMENT STRATEGY AND LENDING PROCESS

## 4.1 Overview of Private Credit Market Opportunity

The private credit market in the United States has grown substantially in recent years, driven by structural changes in the banking industry and persistent demand for short-term, asset-backed financing among real estate investors, small business owners, and entrepreneurs. Traditional banks have significantly curtailed their participation in short-term bridge lending, non-owner-occupied residential financing, fix-and-flip loans, and small-balance commercial loans due to increased regulatory capital requirements, heightened credit risk aversion, and operational constraints.

This retrenchment has created a significant financing gap that private credit providers are well-positioned to fill. Borrowers seeking short-term capital for property acquisitions, renovations, refinancing, or working capital needs frequently require speed of execution, flexible underwriting, and certainty of closing — attributes that traditional bank lenders often cannot provide. These borrowers are willing to pay premium interest rates and origination fees in exchange for reliable, fast, and flexible financing.

The General Partner believes that the current market environment presents a compelling opportunity to deploy capital into secured, short-duration loans with attractive risk-adjusted yields. By focusing on a defined geographic region with favorable economic and demographic tailwinds, maintaining disciplined underwriting standards, and diversifying across loan types and borrowers, the Fund seeks to generate consistent interest income and capital preservation for its Limited Partners.

## 4.2 Investment Strategy

The Fund will originate and manage three primary categories of secured loans:

### 4.2.1 Bridge Loans

**Loan Size:** Variable, subject to the 20% single-loan concentration limit.

**Term:** Six (6) to twelve (12) months.

**Interest Rate:** Ten percent (10%) to fourteen percent (14%) per annum.

**Origination Fee:** Two (2) to three (3) points (percentage of the loan amount), collected at closing.

**Collateral:** First-lien position on real property.

**Loan-to-Value:** Sixty-five percent (65%) to seventy-five percent (75%) of the appraised value.

**Use of Proceeds by Borrower:** Bridge loans are typically used by borrowers to acquire real property quickly, refinance an existing short-term obligation, or bridge the gap between a purchase and the availability of permanent financing. Bridge loan borrowers typically expect to repay the loan through a sale of the property, a refinance with a conventional lender, or other exit strategy within the loan term.

### 4.2.2 Renovation/Rehabilitation Loans

**Loan Size:** Variable, subject to the 20% single-loan concentration limit.

**Term:** Three (3) months base term, with extensions available at the General Partner's discretion.

**Interest Rate:** Twelve percent (12%) to fifteen percent (15%) per annum.

**Origination Fee:** Two (2) to four (4) points, collected at closing.

**Collateral:** First-lien position on real property.

**Loan-to-Value:** Up to seventy percent (70%) of the after-repair value ("ARV") of the property.

**Disbursement:** Loan proceeds may be disbursed in draws based on completion milestones as determined by the General Partner. The Fund may retain a portion of the loan amount in a holdback reserve to be disbursed upon verification of completion of specified renovation milestones.

**Use of Proceeds by Borrower:** Renovation loans are used by borrowers to finance the acquisition and rehabilitation of residential or commercial properties. Borrowers typically plan to sell the renovated property or refinance with permanent financing upon completion of the renovation.

### 4.2.3 Micro Loans

**Loan Size:** Ten thousand dollars ($10,000) to seventy-five thousand dollars ($75,000).

**Term:** Three (3) months base term.

**Interest Rate:** Fourteen percent (14%) to eighteen percent (18%) per annum.

**Origination Fee:** Three (3) to five (5) points, collected at closing.

**Collateral:** Real property or other qualifying assets as determined by the General Partner.

**Loan-to-Value:** As determined by the General Partner based on collateral type and borrower risk profile.

**Use of Proceeds by Borrower:** Micro loans are designed for small-balance financing needs, including but not limited to property deposits, minor renovations, working capital for real estate transactions, and other short-term capital requirements.

## 4.3 Underwriting Criteria

The General Partner will apply rigorous underwriting standards to all loans originated by the Fund. While each loan will be evaluated on its individual merits, the General Partner will generally consider the following factors in its underwriting process:

**(a) Collateral Analysis.** The General Partner will evaluate the quality, condition, location, and market value of the collateral securing each loan. For real property collateral, the General Partner will generally obtain an independent appraisal, broker price opinion, or other third-party valuation. The General Partner will evaluate the property's current condition, comparable sales, rental income potential (if applicable), environmental considerations, and zoning/land use compliance.

**(b) Borrower Analysis.** The General Partner will evaluate each borrower's financial condition, creditworthiness, experience, and track record. Factors considered may include credit history and scores, assets and liabilities, income and cash flow, experience with similar projects, prior loan performance, and character references.

**(c) Loan Structure.** The General Partner will structure each loan to protect the Fund's interests, including appropriate interest rates, origination fees, term limits, personal guarantees (where applicable), insurance requirements, title insurance, and protective covenants. The General Partner will generally require a first-priority lien on the collateral.

**(d) Exit Strategy.** The General Partner will evaluate the borrower's proposed plan for repayment of the loan, including the feasibility and timing of the borrower's exit strategy (sale of the property, refinancing, or other source of repayment).

**(e) Loan-to-Value.** The General Partner will generally adhere to the target LTV ratios set forth above. No loan will be originated at an LTV ratio that the General Partner believes presents an unacceptable risk of loss to the Fund, taking into account all relevant factors.

## 4.4 Loan-to-Value Guidelines

The Fund's LTV guidelines are designed to provide a margin of safety in the event of borrower default and collateral liquidation:

| Loan Type | Maximum LTV |
|---|---|
| Bridge Loans | 65%–75% of appraised value |
| Renovation/Rehab Loans | 70% of after-repair value (ARV) |
| Micro Loans | Determined on a case-by-case basis |

The General Partner may, in its discretion, originate loans with LTV ratios outside these ranges in exceptional circumstances where the General Partner believes the overall risk profile of the loan is acceptable, taking into account factors such as the borrower's creditworthiness, the nature and quality of the collateral, additional guarantees or cross-collateralization, and other risk mitigants.

## 4.5 Geographic Focus

The Fund will focus its lending activities in the following southern belt states: Texas, Tennessee, North Carolina, Georgia, Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Arkansas, South Carolina, and Louisiana.

The General Partner has selected these target markets based on the following factors:

- **Population Growth.** The southern belt states have consistently experienced above-average population growth, driven by domestic migration from higher-cost states, job creation, and quality-of-life factors.

- **Economic Expansion.** These states are benefiting from diversified economic growth across sectors including technology, healthcare, logistics, manufacturing, and energy.

- **Housing Demand.** Strong population growth and household formation are driving demand for residential properties, creating opportunities for borrowers engaged in property acquisition, renovation, and development — and, in turn, demand for the type of short-term financing the Fund provides.

- **Favorable Pricing Dynamics.** Private credit in these markets commands attractive interest rates and origination fees, reflecting borrower demand for speed and flexibility that traditional lenders cannot match.

- **Regulatory Environment.** The target states generally maintain business-friendly regulatory environments, including lending laws that support private credit operations.

The General Partner reserves the right to originate loans outside these target states on an opportunistic basis, provided that such loans meet the Fund's underwriting standards and risk parameters.

## 4.6 Due Diligence Process

The General Partner will conduct thorough due diligence on each prospective loan, including but not limited to the following:

1. **Application Review.** Receipt and review of a complete loan application, including borrower financial statements, project description, and proposed use of proceeds.

2. **Credit Analysis.** Review of borrower credit reports, background checks, and prior loan performance.

3. **Collateral Valuation.** Engagement of independent appraisers or utilization of broker price opinions, automated valuation models, or other valuation methodologies to determine collateral value.

4. **Title Search and Insurance.** Engagement of a title company to conduct a title search, resolve any title defects, and issue title insurance in favor of the Fund.

5. **Property Inspection.** For renovation loans, the General Partner or its agents will conduct an on-site or virtual inspection of the property to assess its current condition and evaluate the feasibility of the proposed renovation scope and budget.

6. **Legal Review.** Review of all loan documents by the Fund's legal counsel to ensure compliance with applicable laws and proper documentation of the Fund's security interest.

7. **Environmental Review.** Where appropriate, the General Partner will require an environmental assessment or review of available environmental data to identify potential contamination or other environmental risks.

8. **Insurance Verification.** Verification that the borrower maintains adequate property insurance, with the Fund named as mortgagee or additional insured.

## 4.7 Loan Monitoring and Servicing

Following the origination of each loan, the General Partner (or its servicing affiliate, NexusBridge Lending LLC) will actively monitor and service the Fund's loan portfolio, including:

- **Payment Monitoring.** Tracking all scheduled payments of interest and principal and promptly identifying delinquencies.

- **Collateral Monitoring.** Periodic review of collateral values and conditions, particularly for renovation loans where the General Partner will inspect renovation progress at milestone disbursement points.

- **Borrower Communication.** Maintaining regular communication with borrowers regarding loan status, payment obligations, extension requests, and any operational issues.

- **Default Management.** Promptly addressing loan defaults through borrower workout negotiations, loan modifications, forbearance agreements, or, where necessary, foreclosure proceedings. The General Partner's objective is to maximize recovery for the Fund in all default situations.

- **Investor Reporting.** Providing quarterly reports to Limited Partners summarizing the Fund's loan portfolio, performance metrics, distributions, and other relevant information.

## 4.8 Exit and Repayment

The Fund's loans are short-duration in nature, with terms generally ranging from three (3) to twelve (12) months. The primary sources of loan repayment are:

- **Property Sale.** The borrower sells the underlying property and repays the loan from the sale proceeds.

- **Refinancing.** The borrower obtains permanent or longer-term financing from a bank, credit union, or other lender and repays the Fund's loan from the refinancing proceeds.

- **Other Sources.** The borrower repays the loan from other available cash flow, asset liquidation, or other sources.

Upon repayment of a loan, the General Partner will evaluate whether to redeploy the capital into new loans (during the Fund's investment period) or distribute the capital to Limited Partners in accordance with the distribution waterfall. During any wind-down or extension period, the General Partner will generally distribute repaid capital to Limited Partners rather than originating new loans, unless the General Partner determines that redeployment is in the best interest of the Fund and its Limited Partners.

Distributable proceeds of the Fund shall be distributed to the partners in the following order of priority:

**Step 1 — Return of Capital.** First, one hundred percent (100%) to Limited Partners, pro rata in proportion to their respective capital contributions, until each Limited Partner has received cumulative distributions equal to such Limited Partner's aggregate capital contributions.

**Step 2 — Preferred Return.** Second, one hundred percent (100%) to Limited Partners, pro rata in proportion to their respective capital contributions, until each Limited Partner has received a cumulative, non-compounded preferred return equal to eight percent (8%) per annum on such Limited Partner's unreturned capital contributions, calculated from the date each capital contribution was made to the date of distribution.

**Step 3 — General Partner Catch-Up.** Third, one hundred percent (100%) to the General Partner, until the General Partner has received cumulative distributions under this Step 3 equal to twenty percent (20%) of the aggregate amount distributed to Limited Partners under Step 2 (the preferred return).

**Step 4 — Residual Split.** Fourth, eighty percent (80%) to Limited Partners (pro rata in proportion to their respective capital contributions) and twenty percent (20%) to the General Partner.

For the avoidance of doubt, the distribution waterfall operates on a "whole fund" basis across the entire Fund, not on a deal-by-deal basis. The General Partner shall not receive any distributions under Steps 3 or 4 until all Limited Partners have received distributions equal to their capital contributions plus the preferred return.

## 11.2 Definition of "Accredited Investor"

Under Rule 501(a) of Regulation D (as amended through the date of this Memorandum), an "accredited investor" includes any person who comes within one or more of the following categories at the time of the sale of the securities:

**(1) Institutional Investors.** Any bank as defined in Section 3(a)(2) of the Securities Act, or any savings and loan association or other institution as defined in Section 3(a)(5)(A) of the Securities Act, whether acting in its individual or fiduciary capacity; any broker or dealer registered pursuant to Section 15 of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934; any investment adviser registered pursuant to Section 203 of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 or registered pursuant to the laws of a state, or any investment adviser relying on the exemption from registering with the SEC under Section 203(l) or 203(m) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940; any insurance company as defined in Section 2(a)(13) of the Securities Act; any investment company registered under the Investment Company Act of 1940 or a business development company as defined in Section 2(a)(48) of that Act; any Small Business Investment Company licensed by the U.S. Small Business Administration under Section 301(c) or (d) of the Small Business Investment Act of 1958; any Rural Business Investment Company as defined in Section 384A of the Consolidated Farm and Rural Development Act; any plan established and maintained by a state, its political subdivisions, or any agency or instrumentality of a state or its political subdivisions, for the benefit of its employees, if such plan has total assets in excess of $5,000,000; any employee benefit plan within the meaning of the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 if the investment decision is made by a plan fiduciary, as defined in Section 3(21) of such Act, which is either a bank, savings and loan association, insurance company, or registered investment adviser, or if the employee benefit plan has total assets in excess of $5,000,000, or if it is a self-directed plan, the investment decisions are made solely by persons that are accredited investors.

**(2) Private Business Development Companies.** Any private business development company as defined in Section 202(a)(22) of the Investment Advisers Act of 1940.

**(3) Charitable Organizations, Corporations, Partnerships, LLCs, and Trusts.** Any organization described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, corporation, Massachusetts or similar business trust, partnership, or limited liability company, not formed for the specific purpose of acquiring the securities offered, with total assets in excess of $5,000,000.

**(4) Directors, Executive Officers, and General Partners.** Any director, executive officer, or general partner of the issuer of the securities being offered or sold, or any director, executive officer, or general partner of a general partner of that issuer.

**(5) Natural Persons — Net Worth Test.** Any natural person whose individual net worth, or joint net worth with that person's spouse or spousal equivalent, exceeds $1,000,000, excluding the value of the person's primary residence. For purposes of this test, the value of a person's primary residence is not included as an asset, and indebtedness secured by the primary residence up to the estimated fair market value of the home is not included as a liability (except that any increase in the amount of indebtedness secured by the primary residence in the sixty (60) days preceding the purchase of the Interests shall be included as a liability).

**(6) Natural Persons — Income Test.** Any natural person who had individual income in excess of $200,000 in each of the two most recent years, or joint income with that person's spouse or spousal equivalent in excess of $300,000 in each of those years, and has a reasonable expectation of reaching the same income level in the current year.

**(7) Trusts.** Any trust, with total assets in excess of $5,000,000, not formed for the specific purpose of acquiring the securities offered, whose purchase is directed by a sophisticated person as described in Rule 506(b)(2)(ii) of Regulation D; or any revocable trust where each grantor is an accredited investor.

**(8) Entities Owned Entirely by Accredited Investors.** Any entity in which all of the equity owners are accredited investors.

**(9) Knowledgeable Employees of Private Funds.** Any natural person who is a "knowledgeable employee" of the Fund, as defined in Rule 3c-5(a)(4) under the Investment Company Act, with respect to the Fund.

**(10) SEC and State-Registered Investment Advisers.** Any investment adviser registered pursuant to Section 203 of the Investment Advisers Act or registered pursuant to the laws of a state, and any investment adviser relying on the exemption from registration under Section 203(l) or 203(m) of the Investment Advisers Act.

**(11) Licensed Securities Professionals.** Any natural person holding in good standing one or more of the following professional certifications, designations, or credentials: the General Securities Representative license (Series 7), the Private Securities Offerings Representative license (Series 82), or the Licensed Investment Adviser Representative (Series 65), as administered by the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority ("FINRA").

**(13) Spousal Equivalents.** For purposes of the income and net worth tests described above, the term "spousal equivalent" means a cohabitant occupying a relationship generally equivalent to that of a spouse.

## 11.3 Verification of Accredited Investor Status

Because this offering is conducted under Rule 506(c), the General Partner is required to take "reasonable steps to verify" that each purchaser of Interests is an accredited investor. Self-certification alone is not sufficient.

The General Partner may employ one or more of the following verification methods, as applicable:

**(a) Income Verification.** Reviewing copies of any Internal Revenue Service form that reports the investor's income for the two most recent years (including, but not limited to, Form W-2, Form 1099, Schedule K-1 to Form 1065, and Form 1040) and obtaining a written representation from the investor that the investor has a reasonable expectation of reaching the required income level for the current year.

**(b) Net Worth Verification.** Reviewing one or more of the following types of documentation dated within the prior three (3) months: bank statements; brokerage statements and other statements of securities holdings; certificates of deposit; tax assessments and appraisal reports issued by independent third parties; and a consumer credit report from at least one of the nationwide consumer reporting agencies. The General Partner will also obtain a written representation from the investor regarding all liabilities necessary to make a net worth determination.

**(c) Third-Party Verification.** Obtaining a written confirmation from a registered broker-dealer, an SEC-registered investment adviser, a licensed attorney, or a certified public accountant that such person or entity has taken reasonable steps to verify that the investor is an accredited investor within the prior three (3) months and has determined that the investor is an accredited investor.

**(d) Existing Investors.** For investors who previously invested in the Fund as accredited investors and remain investors, obtaining a current written certification from such investor at the time of any additional investment that the investor continues to qualify as an accredited investor.

**(e) Entities.** For entities claiming accredited investor status based on total assets or based on all equity owners being accredited investors, reviewing organizational documents, financial statements, or other evidence of assets, and (if applicable) verifying the accredited investor status of each equity owner.

The General Partner reserves the right to require such additional documentation or information as it deems necessary or appropriate to verify an investor's accredited investor status.
